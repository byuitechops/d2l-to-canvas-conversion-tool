General Lesson Notes

PSYCH 111 - Psychology and Scientific Thinking

General Course Overview

This introductory Psychology course is divided into lessons by week. Each lesson consists of a number of activities and an exam. Some lessons include a project, essay, or other large assignment. There is also a comprehensive final exam at the end of the course.

Weekly Online Meeting

You have 60 minutes to hold an online class meeting in each lesson. You may hold this meeting at any time during the week. What you do in this meeting is up to you; however, there are suggestions for discussion topics in some of the learning activities.

Record each online class meeting and post the link to the recording immediately so students who cannot attend will be able to view the recording and complete the meeting report.

Note

If you would rather not hold a 60-minute online meeting, you can to do whatever you want with this 60 minutes. For example, you might post a 30-minute lecture and hold a 30-minute meeting. You could also meet with students in small groups for short periods of time that add up to 60 minutes. Essentially, you can use this time in whatever way you think will help students the most.

Course Maintenance

Fixes

A fix is a correction to errors in course content or design that may have appeared through human error. Even though errors may be identified by any instructor, many fixes will need approval by the Online Course Representative (OCR) before they are implemented. The OCR is familiar enough with both course function and design to recognize and know the proper fix for mechanical and content errors. Examples of fixes include broken links, date changes, grammar/spelling errors, and audio/video issues.

To submit a fix ticket, use the Course Fixes and Improvements form located in the Instructor Resources folder. Once a ticket is received, Course Improvement employees will update the Reference course for next semester. You might need to make the corrections to your current section. Course Improvement and your OCR can help you make those corrections, if needed.

Improvements

An improvement is a suggested change that did not exist in the initial design of the course. It requires review by and approval from the Course Lead (CL) and the OCR before Course Maintenance can implement it in the course. These changes are usually large-scale and frequently require more time to implement. 

If you have ideas for improving an assignment, instructions, grading rubric, groups, etc. please send your idea to your OCR for consideration. Please do not make these changes in your section, as they need to be discussed by the course council. Those ideas which are approved and authorized will then be implemented in the course.

Weekly Expectations

The recurring tasks listed below usually need to be completed each week.

Before the Lesson Begins

  • Create and post weekly Announcement/send class-wide email introducing the lesson or addressing concerns.
    • Tip: Any class-wide emails should also be posted in your Announcements.

During the Lesson

  • Answer student questions via Questions and Conversations, email, etc.
  • Participate and guide student learning in discussion forums. Ask questions of students to inspire learning and help them learn/discover the answers they are seeking.
  • Reach out to students who might be struggling.

Grading and Feedback

  • Examine student work and provide feedback as needed.
  • Publish scores and mark unsubmitted assignments with a zero.
